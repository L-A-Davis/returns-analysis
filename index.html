Analysis About Improvements

Background and Assumptions

Purpose

To enhance confidence / validation of returns analysis

Background

Often in financial analysis, we end up building complex models with substantial and thoughtful details, breaking down the components of multiple lines of business in order to build them back up again to ultimately see the total picture and make the best decision possible with the information available. After all of that complexity, I wanted to create a way for financial analysts / associates to efficiently check the ultimate outputs prior to their use

Notes on the Model

  • Annual cash flows only
    • No partial years
    • No quarterly or monthly inputs
    • Time period of up to 30 years
  • Major line item totals
    • EBITDA and Capital / Other Costs
    • One growth input for each (can be positive or negative)
    • Use subtotals / average rates as proxy for models with multiple EBITDA and / or other income or expense streams
  • Can set EBITDA to:
    • Current: Amount not reflected in cash flows, year one is based on current * annual growth
    • Forward: Sets input as the first yearâ€™s EBITDA
  • Only one debt component input
    • Take the blended average of terms to ballpark more complex debt structures
    • If no rate is specified and a debt amount is entered, 5% is the default
    • Debt is calculated on a monthly basis and then summarized for cash flow output
    • No refinancing or ability to place additional debt / repay early
  • No early / partial sales of assets
  • Select IRR or XIRR
    • IRR assumes final cash flow and terminal value occur on same date. Shown separately for presentation
    • XIRR assumes midyear convention for annual cash flows, entry at time zero and exit at the end of the final year
    • Will not calculate returns if inputs create significantly punitive or outrageously positive returns (ie. buying for $100 and selling for $10)
    • Returns are calculated on full numbers. Rounding is for presentation

Analysis About Improvements

Additional Future Functionality

Enhancements

This is the first iteration of financial analysis that will be continue to be refined and improved upon

Basic

  • Sensitivity tables
  • Ability to set entry and exit based on multiple or set amount
  • Multiple pieces of debt with different terms
  • Mezz debt and preferred equity
  • Ability to take multiple EBITDA streams and label them accordingly
  • Sources and uses to address current capital structure as well
  • Specific tax calculation
  • Specific line items for capEx and changes in working capital

Intermediate

  • Changing growth rates over time
  • Debt refinancing upon maturity with different assumptions than initial debt
  • Early asset sales and debt repayment
  • Ability to change names for line items
  • Ability to show additional revenue, expense, and overhead detail as desired
  • Mobile-friendly layout

Longer Term

  • Monthly, quarterly analysis, including annual roll ups and partial years
  • Specific input overrides
  • Additional models including:
    • Joint venture with ability to model equity splits / promotes
    • Public company mergers: one and multi-year
    • Fairness Opinion metrics: dividend discount, multiples analysis, WACC, DCF, NAVs
    • Real estate model based upon cap rates
    • Corporate models with ability to buy and sell assets, draw on line of credit, forecast balance sheet, capital raises, and credit compliance
  • Export to excel
  • Chart outputs and basic company overview with capitalization, debt maturities, ability to summarize various revenue streams
  • Link to database / ability to take inputs from specific documents to populate inputs (ie. entering a company ticker would fill in all company-related details)
  • Make dynamic company / deal overviews with outputs in user-friendly format
